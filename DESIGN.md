# Design System — FIFA World Cup 2026 Official Brand Identity

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere

The FIFA World Cup 2026 brand identity is built around a single, infinitely scalable idea: **the "26" numeral as a vessel**. The numeral is not decoration — it is the container. Trophy images, city illustrations, national colors, and cultural patterns are all poured *into* the "26" shape, making every expression of the brand feel both unified and locally distinct.

The construction of "26" is intentionally geometric: every stroke is made from **squares and quarter-circles only**, yielding exactly 48 distinct geometric units — one per competing nation. This isn't arbitrary; it means the numeral *is* the tournament at a structural level. The letterforms feel bold and architectural without any sharp corners or serifs — they read as warm, rounded blocks, not corporate slabs.

The tone is **confident minimalism with a multicolor escape valve**. The primary logo runs in black-and-white with a photorealistic gold trophy — restrained, ceremonial, prestige-coded. But the brand deliberately opens into a full multicolor system for city brands, campaign materials, and fan-facing contexts, where the "26" becomes a rainbow of local expression. This duality — monochrome authority + chromatic festivity — is the entire identity strategy.

The campaign slogan `WE ARE 26` extends the typographic logic: the same chunky, geometric sans is used for copy, making text and logo feel like the same object at different scales.

**Key Characteristics:**
- "26" built from 48 geometric units (squares + quarter-circles only) — ball geometry + pitch geometry
- Two-mode system: **Monochrome primary** (black, white, gold) + **Multicolor flexible** (city/fan expressions)
- Photorealistic World Cup trophy — always rendered as a photograph, never as an illustration
- FWC 2026: custom geometric sans-serif, weight 900 equivalent, with diagonally-cut horizontal bars on "F"
- Noto Sans as secondary system font — global language support
- "26" as window device: city illustrations, patterns, and flags fill the numeral's interior
- WE ARE 26 campaign: typographically styled to match the logo system exactly
- Concentric/tunnel multicolor pattern: "26" repeated at scale, each copy in a different color band, creating a chromatic depth effect (the key decorative motif in the album and campaign)
- Zero gradients on type — all flat, solid fills
- Host city logos: same structure, local colors and cultural motifs fill the "26" interior

### Font Families

| Role         | Typeface          | Notes                                                                    |
|--------------|-------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Display**  | FWC 2026 (custom) | Proprietary. Geometric sans, weight 900, diagonally-cut "F" crossbars. Closest open-source alternatives: `Nula Heavy`, `Burlingame Pro Condensed Black`, or `Bebas Neue` for the condensed feel. |

| Legal / Caption   | Noto Sans | 11–13px     | 400      | Sentence | 0              | 1.60        | `#666` on white                     |

### Type Principles
- **FWC 2026 is for the "26" and campaign headlines only** — never for body copy or UI
- **ALL CAPS is mandatory for FWC 2026** — it was designed exclusively for uppercase setting
- **Line-height 0.88–0.90** on display sizes — the density is intentional, letters sit tightly
- **Noto Sans carries everything else** — its global character set is a feature, not a fallback
- **Diagonal cut on "F"** — the horizontal crossbars of "F" are cut at an angle, giving the wordmark kinetic tension. Replicate this detail when building SVG versions.
- **No italic** anywhere in the official system

## 4. The "26" Construction — Core Logo Rules

The "26" is the most important single asset in the system. These rules are non-negotiable:

```
Geometry:
  - Built exclusively from: rectangles + quarter-circles
  - Total geometric units: 48 (referencing 48 teams)
  - Corners: all interior corners are quarter-circles, not sharp
  - Stroke weight: uniform — no thin/thick contrast (not a serif)
  - The "2": one rectangle (top) + one quarter-circle (top-right) + one rectangle (diagonal) + one quarter-circle (bottom-left)
  - The "6": one circle (top loop) + one rectangle (stem) — the loop is a full circle, not an oval

Stacking:
  - "2" sits directly above "6" — vertically aligned, not side-by-side
  - Combined height: ~2:1 ratio to width
  - Inner counter of "6" (the enclosed circular space): this is where the FIFA wordmark lives

Color modes:
  Mode A — Monochrome: "26" fills in black (#000000), white background
  Mode B — City color: "26" interior filled with local color/illustration, outline remains black
  Mode C — Multicolor tunnel: "26" repeated concentrically, each at a different spectrum color
  Mode D — White reverse: "26" in white on dark/colored background

Trophy placement:
  - Always photorealistic (photograph, not illustration)
  - Placed at the intersection of "2" and "6" — straddling the vertical center
  - Scale: trophy occupies approximately 40–50% of the combined "26" height
  - Trophy always faces forward, vertically centered
```

## 5. The Tunnel / Concentric Pattern

This is the primary decorative system — used extensively in the Panini album, broadcast backgrounds, and campaign visuals.

```
Construction:
  1. Start with the "26" at its base size (e.g. 200px tall)
  2. Scale it up 115% → assign the next spectrum color
  3. Scale up another 115% → next spectrum color
  4. Repeat until the frame is filled
  5. Each "26" is centered on the same point — creating a tunnel effect
  6. The foreground "26" is always black, innermost ring is always mint/aqua (#7EEBD0)
  7. Hard edges between bands — no blur, no feathering

Color sequence (innermost → outermost):
  Mint (#7EEBD0) → Deep Forest (#0D4A28) → Emerald (#1A7A46) → Mid Green (#3AB84A)
  → Lime (#A8D830) → Yellow (#F5D020) → Coral (#E87C6A) → Red (#C42020)
  → Maroon (#7A1A1A) → Violet (#7B3FA8) → Cobalt (#2E5FBF) → Sky Blue (#6EC6E8)

Application:
  - Always used as a full-bleed background — never as a contained element
  - The black "26" foreground is always placed centered over the tunnel
  - Never use the tunnel pattern on small surfaces (below 400px wide)
  - The pattern always reads as symmetric — equal margins on all 4 sides
```

## 6. Component Stylings

### Logo Lockup — Standard
```
[Trophy photograph]
    [26 — black, geometric]
  [FIFA — all-caps, tracked]
[OFFICIAL LICENSED PRODUCT — 9px, tracked wide]

Background: White (#FFFFFF) OR transparent
Container: No border, no pill, no card — the lockup floats freely
Minimum size: 120px tall
Clear space: equal to the height of the "F" in FIFA on all sides
```

### Logo Lockup — Dark Pill (Panini album badge)
```
Background: #000000 rounded rectangle (border-radius: ~16% of width)
"26": White, geometric
"FIFA": White, all-caps, tracked
Trophy: photorealistic, centered on "26" junction
Padding: 12% of container width on all sides
```

### City Logo Variant
```
Same structure as standard lockup
The interior of "26" is filled with the city's local color + cultural illustration
The outline of "26" remains black
Each city has one assigned color from the host-country family (USA blues, MEX greens, CAN reds)
```

### Buttons

**Primary (Black)**
- Background: `#000000`
- Text: `#FFFFFF`, Noto Sans 600, ALL CAPS, letter-spacing 0.06em
- Padding: `12px 32px`
- Radius: `4px` — **not a pill**. The brand uses restrained corners, not pill buttons.
- Hover: background softens to `#1A1A1A`, scale `1.02`
- Active: scale `0.98`

**Secondary (Outline)**
- Background: `transparent`
- Border: `2px solid #000000`
- Text: `#000000`
- Same padding, radius, scale animation

**City Accent (filled with city color)**
- Background: city's assigned color (e.g. `#1A3A8F` for a US city)
- Text: `#FFFFFF`
- Same specs otherwise

### Cards & Containers
- Radius: `8px` (small), `16px` (medium), `24px` (large feature)
- Border: `1.5px solid rgba(0,0,0,0.12)`
- Background: `#FFFFFF`
- No drop shadow — depth comes from border and color contrast
- Feature cards: use a band of spectrum color as a top border accent (`4px solid [spectrum color]`)

### Sticker Card (Panini)
- Background: `#89C8F5` (baby blue — not white, not the FIFA primary)
- "26" watermark: oversized, `rgba(255,255,255,0.08)`, bottom-right, slightly rotated
- Player photo: cutout, positioned over a national-color tint layer
- Flag + FIFA code bar: `#0A1E4A` (deep navy), height 24px
- Name + metadata bar: `#1557A8` (FIFA blue), player name 700 weight, metadata 400 smaller
- Club bar: same `#1557A8`, lighter metadata text
- Border radius: `10px`

## 7. Layout Principles

### Spacing System
- Base unit: 8px
- Scale: 4 · 8 · 12 · 16 · 24 · 32 · 48 · 64 · 96 · 128 · 192px

### Border Radius Scale

| Usage                    | Value   |
|--------------------------|---------|
| Buttons, inputs          | `4px`   |
| Tags, chips              | `4px`   |
| Cards                    | `8px`   |
| Feature cards            | `16px`  |
| Logo badge (dark pill)   | `16%` of width |
| "26" numeral interior    | Quarter-circle units only |
| Pill shapes              | Never — not part of this system |

### Grid

| Context         | Columns | Notes                                         |
|-----------------|---------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Hero / banner   | 1       | Centered "26" + tunnel pattern full-bleed     |
| Feature section | 2       | Text left, visual right (or city logo)        |
| Album page      | 4–5     | Sticker grid, equal slots                     |
| City logos grid | 3–4     | Each city logo at equal size                  |
| Mobile          | 1       | Stack, tunnel pattern at reduced scale        |

### Composition Rules
- **"26" is always the dominant element** — it should be the largest visible thing in any composition
- **Trophy always breaks out of its container** — it sits ON the "26", not inside a box
- **Campaign text sits BELOW the logo** at a comfortable 1/4 of the logo height in gap
- **Tunnel pattern never fights with foreground content** — always enough contrast

## 8. Depth & Elevation

| Level      | Treatment                                  | Use                              |
|------------|--------------------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Flat (0)   | No shadow, no border                       | Type, logo, tunnel pattern       |
| Ring (1)   | `1.5px solid rgba(0,0,0,0.12)`             | Cards, input fields              |
| Accent (2) | `4px solid [spectrum color]` top border    | Featured cards                   |
| Badge (3)  | Black fill + white content                 | Logo badge, Panini badge         |

**Shadow philosophy:** Zero drop shadows. Depth is expressed through the tunnel pattern (infinite perceived depth), color contrast (black logo on white), and the physical emergence of the photorealistic trophy. Shadows would flatten the effect.

## 9. Motion & Animation

- **Tunnel entrance:** each "26" ring fades in from outside inward — stagger 60ms per ring, ease-out 0.5s. Creates the sensation of the tunnel opening toward the viewer.
- **Logo reveal:** trophy scales in from 0.85 → 1.0, opacity 0 → 1, over 0.6s ease-out. The "26" is already present before the trophy arrives.
- **City color fill:** the interior of "26" floods with city color using a radial wipe from center, 0.4s ease-in-out.
- **Sticker placement:** scale 0 → 1.08 → 1.0 (spring bounce), 0.3s. The sticker physically lands and settles.
- **Button hover:** scale 1.02, transition 0.15s ease. Active: scale 0.98.
- **No looping animations** on marketing materials — motion is entrance-only, then static.
- **WE ARE 26 campaign text:** letter by letter stagger, 30ms gap, slide up 12px + fade in.

## 10. Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Build the "26" from rectangles and quarter-circles only — never freehand curves
- Use exactly 48 geometric units when constructing the numeral
- Keep the primary logo black-and-white — let the city logos carry the color
- Always use a photographic trophy render — never illustrate or flatten it
- Fill the "26" interior with city-specific content — this is how the system scales
- Use the tunnel/concentric pattern as a full-bleed background
- Keep FWC 2026 exclusively for display — Noto Sans carries all body copy
- Maintain ALL CAPS for anything set in FWC 2026
- Use `4px` border radius on buttons — not pill shapes

### Don't
- Don't add gradients to the "26" or any logo element — solid fills only
- Don't use the tunnel pattern at small sizes — it needs space to read as depth
- Don't illustrate the trophy — it must always be photographic
- Don't use FWC 2026 for body text — it was built for display sizes only
- Don't use pill-shaped buttons — this brand uses squared, restrained corners
- Don't place the trophy inside a box or container — it always floats freely
- Don't use the multicolor palette on official/ceremonial documents — monochrome primary only
- Don't create a new color for a city logo — only use the predefined host-country tones
- Don't add drop shadows anywhere
